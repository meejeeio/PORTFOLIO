About Me

I come from a humble background in a remote village where access to education and healthcare was scarce. Despite these challenges, I remained determined to pursue learning and build a better future for myself and my community. At the age of five, I enrolled in a local government school. Our class was small, and reaching school meant walking nearly three hours each day through rugged Himalayan terrain. With limited resources, few academic opportunities, and the burden of household responsibilities, education was never easy. Still, I persevered and eventually graduated with one of the highest GPAs ever recorded in my region. Recognizing my academic achievements, I was awarded a full scholarship to pursue higher secondary education in Biology at a reputable college in the capital. The transition to a new environment and English-medium education was challenging, but through dedication and hard work, I adapted and continued to perform well academically. Driven by the dream of becoming a doctor and serving underserved communities, I prepared rigorously for the national scholarship entrance and was fortunate to earn a fully funded seat at one of Nepalâ€™s leading medical institutions. Now in my third year of medical school, I am deeply committed to expanding my medical knowledge and engaging in clinical research and innovation. My long-term goal is to bridge the healthcare gap in marginalized areas and contribute meaningfully to improving access to quality medical services.
